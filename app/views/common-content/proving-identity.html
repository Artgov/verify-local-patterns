The government standard for identity verification is GOV.UK Verify. Using GOV.UK Verify, the user can securely prove their identity. The service can also recieve the user's address {{"and date of birth" if age}} from GOV.UK Verify.

Councils should also offer alternative routes to access the service if a user chooses not to use GOV.UK Verify. This should include an online and offline alternatives. See sub-section c, below, for more detail on alternatives.

  • "Unlikely to verify" and "verification failed" routes

    Some users may not have the documents or credit records that GOV.UK Verify needs to prove their identity.

    • GOV.UK Verify asks users questions to work out if they are likely to be verified. If it looks like they won't be verified, they are shown the "unlikely to verify" page.
    • A very small proportion of users will go through past these questions, and GOV.UK Verify will still not be able to verify their identity. These users will be told by their chosen Identity Provider that they couldn't be verified, and then be taken back to GOV.UK, where they will see a similar " verification failed" page. (Step through the example GOV.UK Verify journey as a user who can not be verified.)

    Both these pages should link to the alternative ways to access the service described in sub-section c below.

  • Alternative ways to access the service

    Alternative ways to access the service need to be shown to the user on the "proving identity" page, the "unlikely to verify" page, and the " verification failed" page.

    In the alternative online route, users will need to prove their address {{"and age" if age}} by scanning in documents.

    Alternative routes should also include non-digital ways of accessing the service, which may include:

    • A paper form to print, complete and return to the council
    • A phone number to call to do the application over the phone
    • A place (such as a council office or library) that the user can come and get help completing the application process

    Councils will provide details to GDS of the above alternative ways to access the service when integrating with GOV.UK Verify.
