The government standard for identity verification is GOV.UK Verify. Using GOV.UK Verify, the user can securely prove their identity. The service can also recieve the user's address and date of birth from GOV.UK Verify.

Councils should offer an alternative to GOV.UK Verify as the service is not yet available to all UK citizens. This alternative could be an offline service.

  • If GOV.UK Verify can not verify the user's identity

    Some users may not have the documents or credit records that GOV.UK Verify needs to prove their identity.

    • GOV.UK Verify asks users questions to work out if they are likely to be verified, and if it looks like they won't be, they are shown the "unlikely to verify" page.
    • A very small proportion of users will go through past these questions, but still not be able to verify their identity. These users will see a similar "failed to verify" page.

    Both these pages should link to the council's "non-verify route" - The same alternative route linked to from the "Prove Identity" page. In other words, if a user fails to verify for any reason, GOV.UK Verify should link them to the alternative digital service, which may involve manually scanning in documents for example.

    GOV.UK Verify should also tell the user about any non-digital ways of accessing the service, for example:

    • A paper form to print, complete and return to the council
    • A phone number to call to do the application over the phone
    • A place (such as a council office or library) that the user can come and get help completing the application process

    Councils will provide details of the above alternative ways to access the service when integrating with GOV.UK Verify.
