This section is for users to specify their permit needs:

  • number of permits
  • permit length (6 months or 12 months are recommended options)
  • permit start date

Protecting against permit resale

To prevent people from reselling permits, councils require users to assoicate permits with a vehicle. To do this, users must enter their vehicle registration number.

Additional protection

For high fraud risk areas, councils require users to prove that they own the car for additional protection against permit resale.

In future, councils will be able to access data from DVLA to prove vehicle ownership.

Currently, some councils ask users to scan their V5C, or another document associating the user with the vehicle. This is not a secure method for proving vehicle ownership because the document is not validated against records, so this is not recommended.

Permit price

Price of permits goes up incrementally to deter residents from giving their resident permits to visitors.

In some councils, the permit price also varies depending on the vehicle type, emissions, make or other conditions.

Addtional vehicle description for enforcement officers

Some councils ask for make, model and vehicle colour to give to the enforcement officers. This has not been shown to be an effective way of improving enforcement of permits.
