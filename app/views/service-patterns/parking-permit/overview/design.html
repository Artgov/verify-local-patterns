Journey map

The user journey map is based on extensive user research and service design best practice. To follow the pattern, your service should reflect the user journeys illustrated by the journey map diagram.

How to use the map

The map shows the different routes through the online application users will experience, dependant on their input and choices. It shows how steps and pages link together in the different user journeys.

Controls

Hover over the diagram to show the controls. Use the controls to optimise the map, zoom in and out and select user journey routes layers. The controls show at the bottom of the page when the map is optimised.

Layers

Different layers show different routes users may take through the online application – the different user journeys. Keep the ‘Main journey’ layer on while you explore.

Select layers to see the steps involved in each journey:

  • main journey: a "happy path" through the application
  • alternative online: user chooses to scan and upload or was not verified
  • free permit: user does not have to pay for the permit
  • new address: user adds new address after completing GOV.UK Verify log in or registration
  • unverified address: user’s most recent address isn't yet verified by GOV.UK Verify
  • other permits: user doesn't want a resident’s permit
  • waiting list: there are no more permits available in the parking zone

Clicking on a section name will take you to the relevant section of the pattern guidance.

Prototype links

Clicking on an individual page in the diagram will take you to the relevant prototype page.
