Users need to find out about their entitlement to concessionary travel. They may find out in these ways:

  • word of mouth (by far the most common means of discovery)
  • signage on bus stops and buses
  • local, district, borough and county councils communications
  • online, through local authority or travel websites

Your service should make sure the right information is being provided through these channels, where possible.

Finding the right council website

Users need to get to the right council's website to apply for their pass. They might start by typing 'older person bus pass' or a related term into a search engine, or going to the website of the council they think is their local council's.

Search engine

The older person's bus pass page on GOV.UK should send users to the right council's website when they enter their postcode.

Make sure the postcodes your service provides services for link to the right page on your website and email any corrections to: local-direct-admin@digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk.

Council website

Users may go straight to the website for the council they think they need to get a travel pass from.

Clearly state which area your service provides concessionary travel in, as the user may be in the wrong place.
