What we learnt from testing with users

Early iterations of this page presented users with a significant amount of content, with few sub headings. This resulted in most users simply starting the interaction without reading.

Users become eligible for a pass when they reach the female state pension age. Due to current changes in the female state pension age, the age a user becomes eligible varies with their birth year.

Attempts at explaining this have confused users. Linking to the GOV.UK page that allows users to look up their pension age has also confused users as they do not understand the association between bus passes and pensions.

What we changed as a result

  1. 1

    Include a prominent contact link throughout the journey.

  2. 2

    To address confusion around eligibility, a page for the user to check their eligibility has been added. This is linked from the start page, allowing users to simply enter their date of birth to determine their eligibility. This has removed any confusion resulting from the link with female state pension age.

  3. 3

    The content on this page has been broken up with task specific sub-headings, helping those scanning the page to identify relevant content.

  4. 4

    The content on this page has been broken up with task specific sub-headings, helping those scanning the page to identify relevant content.

  5. 5

    Users are told what to expect in the application process, including proving their identity and providing a digital photograph.

  6. 6

    Users are given links to links to renewals and replacements services.

  7. 7

    Users are told what to do if they need assistance.
